Emotions, Feelings, and the Messy Middle.

AEDEN

I still remember the day I realized I had no idea what I was feeling. I was in my early twenties, going through a tough breakup, and trying to navigate the chaos of my emotions. One minute, I was fine; the next, I was experiencing a nervous breakdown. It was like being on a rollercoaster with no safety harness.

I looked around, I realized I wasn't alone. Friends, family, even strangers on social media – everyone seemed to struggle to understand their emotions. We'd use words like "happy," "sad," or "angry" to describe how we felt, but deep down, we knew it was more complicated than that.

That's when I started to explore the difference between emotions and feelings. It's a distinction that's changed the way I understand myself and the world around me.

Emotions, as I've come to learn, are like intense storms that arise in response to a situation or stimulus. They're automatic, instinctual, and often beyond your control. Think of emotions like fear, anger, or excitement – they can be overwhelming and all-consuming.

Feelings, on the other hand, are like the calm after the storm. They're the mental and emotional states that arise from your experiences, perceptions, and thoughts. Feelings are often more subtle and nuanced than emotions, and they are influenced by your thoughts, beliefs, and values. Think of feelings like happiness, gratitude, or love – they're often more cognitive and intentional than emotions.

The line between emotions and feelings is blurry, and it's easy to get caught in the messy middle. You might feel anxious, but is that an emotion or a feeling? Are you responding to a situation, or are you reacting to your thoughts and perceptions?

As I navigated this messy middle, I realized that understanding the difference between emotions and feelings is not about making a neat distinction. It's developing a deeper awareness of ourselves and the world around us.

It's recognizing that your emotions are valid, but they're not always rational. It's acknowledging that your feelings are complex and can't be reduced to a single word or label. It's being kind to yourself as you navigate this messy middle. It's acknowledging that you're not always going to have the answers, and that's okay.

So, the next time you find yourself caught amid an emotional storm, take a deep breath and try to tune in to your feelings. What are you feeling right now? What emotions are arising? Take a step back, breathe, and reflect on your feelings. What are they telling you? What do they need from you?

You're never alone in this journey. We're all navigating the messy middle together, trying to make sense of our emotions and feelings. And that's okay.
