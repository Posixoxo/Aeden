Understanding Who You Are at Your Core.

AEDEN

Have you ever caught yourself wondering, “Who am I, really?” Maybe it was after a conversation that left you feeling off balance, or perhaps it hit you in a quiet moment when everything felt… a little too still.

We all ask ourselves this at some point, and I think it’s one of the most human questions we can explore. The quest to discover our truest selves isn’t a one-time epiphany—it’s a lifelong journey, constantly unfolding, layer by layer. The more you dig, the more you uncover about your strengths, your weaknesses, your values… and sometimes, what you find surprises you.

"I’m not afraid to be alone." Translation: I ghosted everyone because they dared to suggest I might not be perfect. Look, Susan, you didn't walk away; people just got tired of your 45-minute monologues about how you're "vibrating at a higher frequency now."

Understanding who we truly are doesn’t always come easily. It’s not something you read in a self-help book or find in a tweet. It’s a process—one that evolves as we experience life, face challenges, and celebrate our victories. And the more we discover about ourselves, the more we realize how much it shapes every decision we make.

"I’m not interested in being liked, I’m interested in being respected." Cool. But when someone says “good morning” and you respond with “I don’t do small talk,” it’s not earning respect—it’s earning side-eyes and a possible HR complaint.

So, who are you at your core?

It’s not about the labels you wear—your job titles, your hobbies, or the way you define yourself in small talk. It’s deeper than that. It’s understanding your true essence: the parts of you that influence how you see the world, how you make decisions, and how you treat yourself and others.

Over time, this understanding shifts. We aren’t static creatures; we evolve. The person you were five years ago is not the same person you are today. We grow, we learn, and we uncover parts of ourselves we never even knew existed. And that's a beautiful thing.

Why it matters

Now, you might be thinking, “Okay, that’s great, but how does this impact my everyday life?” Great question. When you begin to understand who you truly are, you start making choices from a place of alignment, not confusion or external pressure. You stop second-guessing yourself, and instead, you make decisions that honor your strengths, acknowledge your weaknesses, and celebrate your values.

When you know your core values, for example, making tough decisions becomes easier. Should I take that new job? Should I stay in this relationship? Should I set this boundary with someone? The clearer your understanding of yourself, the clearer the path forward becomes. And as you start living more authentically, life just feels… more right.

It also means you stop living on autopilot. You become the driver of your own life, instead of letting life happen to you. It’s creating space to ask: Is this the life I really want? And then having the courage to go after it unapologetically.
